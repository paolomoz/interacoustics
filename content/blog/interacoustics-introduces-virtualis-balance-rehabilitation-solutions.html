Interacoustics is happy to announce the Virtualis balance rehabilitation solutions as a new addition to their balance portfolio.

Interacoustics is happy to announce the launch of the Virtualis balance rehabilitation solutions as the newest addition to their balance portfolio.

The new Virtualis solutions harness the power of fully immersive virtual reality, simulating real-life environments and promoting rehabilitation strategies such as adaptation, substitution, and habituation. Patients can train safely in an engaging and motivating environment, while clinicians can monitor and guide their progress, aiming to improve daily function and quality of life.

Jacob Bjerrisgaard, Balance Business Unit Manager says:

“We are excited to partner with Virtualis, who has specialized in solutions for vestibular and balance rehabilitation. This new line of products is a great addition to our current balance portfolio. We are now able to deliver solutions for the entire balance journey: from the diagnosis of vestibular disorders to rehabilitation of people who live with dizziness and balance disorders as well as other disorders that require physiotherapy.”

The Virtualis solutions include software presented in an immersive VR environment used in combination with static or dynamic posturography force plates for personalized assessment and training. The dynamic 360-degree force plate – the Computerized Dynamic Posturography (CDP) system – along with the power of immersive virtual reality, simulates real-life surfaces and stimulates the full balance system, including the otoliths.

In addition to this MotionVR solution, the line of products includes StaticVR posturography force plates for assessment and training of balance disorders, BalanceVR that uses only VR for assessment and training of balance disorders, as well as the PhysioVR solution that uses VR for the rehabilitation of neurologic and orthopedic disorders.

“With these new balance rehabilitation solutions, clinicians can now objectively assess and tailor patient training for efficient and motivating care in the increasingly complex world of rehabilitation”, Jacob Bjerrisgaard says.

Interacoustics will sell the Virtualis line of products worldwide through their strong distribution network of sales offices and external distributors.

Virtualis is a French-based company founded in 2015 by Franck Assaban, Physical Therapist to create innovative rehabilitation tools to improve the quality of care in vestibular and balance rehabilitation.
