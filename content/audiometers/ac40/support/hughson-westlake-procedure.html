The Hughson-Westlake procedure is an automated pure tone audiometry test. During the ascending portion of the tone presentation, the threshold of hearing is determined by requiring the patient to provide 2 out of 3 (or 3 out of 5) correct responses. If the patient responds when the tone is ascending, the test will automatically decrease the level by 10 dB. To record the threshold, the patient needs to respond to the same intensity 2 out of 3 or 3 out of 5 times. Intensity increases occur in 5 dB steps, while intensity decreases happen in 10 dB steps.

Press and hold the Tests button and use the black scroll wheel to select Auto - Hughson Westlake.

Instruct the patient that he will hear pulsed tones, which will vary in intensity and to press the button as soon as the tone is heard. Note If the response button is pressed after the patient heard the tones, the response will not be recorded. Therefore it is important to instruct the patient to press the response button as soon as the tone is heard.

To familiarize the patient with the test, press the Famili and press Play.

To start the test, deselect Famili, ensure to activate Trace and press Play.

The Hughson-Westlake test will automatically store the threshold with 2 out of 3 (or 3 out of 5) ascending responses.

All audiometric test frequencies will be measured automatically.
